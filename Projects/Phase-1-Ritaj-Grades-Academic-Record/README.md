## 📌 Project Overview

This folder contains the **Phase 1 Research and Requirements** deliverables for our Human–Computer Interaction project.

Our project focuses on the **Grades and Academic Record** area of the Birzeit University Ritaj Portal. This part of Ritaj is used by students to check current semester grades, inspect their cumulative academic record, and understand possible issues or discrepancies in course marks.

The goal of Phase 1 was to understand the current user experience, identify usability problems, and define measurable requirements that can guide the redesign in the next phase.

---

## 🎯 Project Scope

The redesign scope is limited to three assigned user flows:

| Flow       | Description                                     |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Flow 1** | Viewing current semester grades                 |
| **Flow 2** | Inspecting the cumulative academic record       |
| **Flow 3** | Spotting and querying a course mark discrepancy |

Other Ritaj areas such as registration, timetable, financial pages, and general academic services are outside the scope of this phase.

## 🔍 Research Methods Used

### 1. Heuristic Evaluation

We evaluated the Ritaj grades and academic record area using **Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics**.
The team identified **11 usability violations** across the three assigned flows, including issues related to:

* Visibility of system status
* Consistency and standards
* Recognition rather than recall
* User control and freedom
* Flexibility and efficiency of use
* Aesthetic and minimalist design

---

### 2. Exploratory Interviews

We conducted interviews with real Ritaj users to understand their experiences with grades, GPA, credit hours, academic progress, and mark discrepancies.

The interview findings helped us identify common pain points such as:

* Unclear status of blank or missing marks
* Difficulty understanding remaining credit hours
* Lack of direct communication path when a mark seems incorrect
* Extra manual work required from students

---

### 3. Personas

Two personas were created based on the interview findings:

| Persona          | Main Need                                                                        |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Layla Hammad** | Needs clear grade status and an easy way to ask about unclear or incorrect marks |
| **Tariq Nasser** | Needs a clear academic record summary, especially remaining credit hours         |

---

### 4. Scenarios

Three scenarios were written to describe realistic use cases for the redesigned system:

1. Viewing current semester grades
2. Inspecting the cumulative academic record
3. Spotting and querying a course mark discrepancy

## ✅ Measurable Usability Requirements

The Phase 1 report defines measurable usability requirements mapped to ISO 9241-11 usability goals, including:

* Students should locate a current-semester grade within 10 seconds.
* Students should locate a grade from a selected semester within 30 seconds.
* Students should reach instructor contact information from a grade row in no more than 2 clicks.
* Students should correctly distinguish between pending, published, and withheld marks using clear labels.
* Students should identify remaining credit hours without manual calculation.
* Students should rate the clarity of the redesigned pages at least 4 out of 5.

---

## 🧠 Key Findings

The main usability problems found in the current Ritaj grades and academic record area were:

1. **Status ambiguity**
   Students often cannot tell whether a mark is pending, missing, withheld, or not uploaded.

2. **One-way communication**
   Students can view marks, but they cannot easily ask about a suspicious or incorrect mark from the same page.

3. **Cognitive and visual load**
   Important academic information is present, but it is not always organized in a clear and efficient way.

These findings directly informed the requirements for the redesign phase.
